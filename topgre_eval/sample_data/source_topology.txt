Point-Set Topology: Selected Results (source chapter for card generation)

A topological space is a set X with a collection of open sets closed under
arbitrary unions and finite intersections. A set is closed if its complement is
open; a set is clopen if it is both open and closed. The closure of a set A is A
together with its limit points, and the interior of A is the largest open set
contained in A. The boundary of A is its closure minus its interior. A set A is
dense in X when its closure is all of X; for example, the rationals are dense in
the real line.

A map is continuous if the preimage of every open set is open; equivalently, the
preimage of every closed set is closed. The composition of continuous maps is
continuous. By the pasting lemma, a map that is continuous on each of two closed
sets covering X and that agrees on their overlap is continuous on X.

A space is compact if every open cover has a finite subcover. The continuous
image of a compact set is compact, and a closed subset of a compact space is
compact. By the Heine-Borel theorem, a subset of Euclidean space is compact if
and only if it is closed and bounded. A continuous real-valued function on a
compact space attains its maximum and minimum.

A space is connected if it is not the union of two disjoint nonempty open sets.
The continuous image of a connected set is connected, and the connected subsets
of the real line are exactly the intervals. Every path-connected space is
connected, though the converse fails: the topologist's sine curve is connected
but not path-connected.

A space is Hausdorff if any two distinct points have disjoint open neighborhoods.
Every metric space is Hausdorff, and in a Hausdorff space a convergent sequence
has a unique limit. A compact subset of a Hausdorff space is closed. A continuous
bijection from a compact space to a Hausdorff space is a homeomorphism.

Compactness and connectedness are topological invariants, so they are preserved
by homeomorphisms. Because the closed interval is compact and the open interval
is not, the two are not homeomorphic.
